THOX MagDisplay

See more. Stay local. Own your data.

A pocket-class companion display for the THOX family. MagStack v3.0 snaps it onto ThoxMini, ThoxMini Air, ThoxClip, or ThoxNova in about a second, and a color e-ink screen shows agent status, cluster health, and mesh identity — drawing effectively zero power between updates. Twelve tactile keys let you navigate without ever touching the host.

One glance, three colorways

Model THOX-MCD-rA1. A 4-inch Spectra 6 color e-paper surface in a precision-machined 6061-T6 aluminum enclosure, with an under-edge light pipe that breathes emerald when the cluster is healthy. Matte Black, Space Gray, and Arctic White.

The user journey — snap, glance, navigate
01 · Snap

Align in ~1 second

The circular MagStack v3.0 contact ring aligns and locks to any compatible THOX face. Eight N52 magnets set the polarity; a Hall switch wakes the display on dock and deep-sleeps it on undock.

02 · Glance

Always-on e-ink

Spectra 6 color e-paper holds the image with zero power between refreshes. See system health, a live cluster ring, and your mesh ID at a glance — full refresh in 12–18s, partial in 3–5s.

03 · Navigate

12-key tactile cluster

A 4×4 tactile matrix drives the whole UI without touching the host device — transcripts, audio queue, cluster view — over a 12 Mbps MagStack link. NFC handles secure mesh pairing.

Works across the THOX family — MagStack v3.0

Every THOX device that ships with a MagStack v3.0 face pairs cleanly, with no host-side firmware update — the protocol is versioned at the link layer and negotiates capability at link-up.

Host deviceRole of the MagDisplay
ThoxMiniGlanceable status, agent transcripts, audio queue
ThoxMini AirSame as ThoxMini, reduced refresh rate to conserve power
ThoxClipWearable companion view when undocked, full-rate transcript when docked
ThoxNovaSecondary display surface; anchor node for clusters up to eight peers
Future devicesAny face exposing a MagStack v3.0 ring inherits compatibility
Anatomy

Front glass and display module, color e-ink panel, ThoxOS compute board, NFC controller, battery module, midframe, rear shell, and the MagStack interface ring — eight parts, engineered to stack.

MagStack v3.0 interface — the rear

On the roadmap — not a Kickstarter reward

The MagDisplay is an upcoming companion product, expected to launch early Spring 2027. It is shown here so backers can see where the THOX family is going. The four launch devices — ThoxKey, ThoxMini Air, ThoxMini, ThoxClip — and the MeshStack app are what ship first. Privacy follows the THOX house rules: the MagStack link carries encrypted traffic only, NFC never exposes the long-term mesh key, and the backend never sees plaintext.
