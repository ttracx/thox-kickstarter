## Why we are building this

AI is becoming part of every workflow. But for many people, the most valuable work is also the most sensitive work: client notes, research, strategy, code, legal drafts, product ideas, and personal conversations.

We believe people should be able to use AI without giving up control of their data by default.

THOX.ai is a local-first hardware and software ecosystem for private AI. Smaller devices help you carry identity, route commands, capture context, and trigger workflows. Larger local machines handle heavier workloads. The whole system is designed around a simple rule:

**Use the device for what it can honestly do. Route the rest to hardware the user owns.**

## Meet the devices

[ASSET: four-panel device lineup graphic.]

### ThoxKey

ThoxKey is the USB device for your private AI identity. It carries encrypted configuration, recovery profile, local launcher settings, and the portable THOX setup that lets you move between trusted machines.

Use ThoxKey when you want a simple, affordable way to start using THOX.ai without buying a full device kit.

What ThoxKey is for:

- carrying your THOX identity and configuration
- unlocking your private AI workspace on trusted machines
- restoring settings after a rebuild
- keeping launch and recovery workflows portable

What ThoxKey is not:

- it is not a standalone LLM computer
- it is not a cloud subscription
- it is not a generic storage stick marketed as AI

### ThoxMini Air

ThoxMini Air is the low-cost wireless companion for local-first AI workflows. It connects nearby THOX devices, routes lightweight commands, and helps bridge your private AI workspace.

Use ThoxMini Air when you want an accessible sub-$100 device in the THOX ecosystem.

What ThoxMini Air is for:

- lightweight local routing
- wireless companion workflows
- simple device-to-device coordination
- experimentation with private AI edge setups

What ThoxMini Air is not:

- it is not a replacement for a high-end workstation
- it is not marketed as a large-model inference box

### ThoxMini

ThoxMini is the compact local compute node. It is built for local services, lightweight agents, encrypted storage, and user-owned automations.

Use ThoxMini when you want a practical local-first box for a desk, lab, field kit, or personal workflow.

What ThoxMini is for:

- lightweight local agents
- encrypted local services
- personal automations
- private workflow experiments
- developer and maker workflows

What ThoxMini is not:

- it is not a promise that every large model can run on tiny hardware
- it is not a locked appliance

### ThoxClip

ThoxClip is the premium clip-on command and capture device for private AI workflows. It is designed for fast voice capture, workflow triggers, field notes, and secure handoff into your THOX environment.

Use ThoxClip when you want your AI workflow available when you are away from the desk.

What ThoxClip is for:

- quick capture
- private notes
- workflow triggers
- field and meeting context
- routing captured context to your THOX stack

What ThoxClip is not:

- it is not a medical device
- it is not an emergency response device
- it is not a regulated safety product

## How the THOX flow works

[ASSET: topology graphic.]

1. Carry your identity and configuration with ThoxKey.
2. Connect your local workspace with ThoxMini Air.
3. Run local services and lightweight agents on ThoxMini.
4. Capture and control workflows with ThoxClip.
5. Route heavier work to capable local THOX nodes or user-owned computers.

This is local-first AI hardware with honest boundaries.

## Risks and challenges

Hardware has real risks. The most important risks for this campaign are supplier availability, enclosure fit, certification timing, firmware stability, logistics, and international shipping complexity.

Our plan is to reduce those risks by keeping the first product set focused, avoiding unrealistic AI claims, validating each device independently, using conservative delivery windows, and communicating clearly when tradeoffs appear.

If a delivery date slips, backers will receive a direct update that explains what changed, why it changed, what we are doing next, and when the next update will arrive.
