# Runbook 02: Mid-campaign (T+8 to T+21)

The 14-day grind. Goal: sustain $5K/day baseline, build trust with
mid-funnel backers, set up the stretch push. Cadence drops to 1 post
per day per platform; the team needs the rest.

## Daily template

| Time (PT) | Action | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| 9:00am | Morning post (theme rotates by day, see below) + reply window | Content producer + community manager |
| 1:00pm | Reply window | Community manager |
| 5:00pm | Evening post (if 2x/day cadence) + reply window | Producer + community manager |
| 8:30pm | Schedule tomorrow's post in Buffer | Content producer |

## Weekly content rotation (1 post/day per platform)

The mid-campaign drumbeat is a weekly rotation. Repeats each week.

| Day | Theme | Hero | Platform priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mon | Backer spotlight | UGC repost or backer-submitted photo + thank-you | All |
| Tue | Device close-up (rotate Clip/Mini/Air/Nova week to week) | Device hero shot + 4-second loop | IG + TikTok |
| Wed | Stretch progress update | Live thermometer graphic | All |
| Thu | Technical deep-dive | Carousel or thread + diagram | LinkedIn + X |
| Fri | Behind-the-scenes | Shop walkthrough, calibration print, prototype iteration | IG + TikTok |
| Sat | Community highlight | Tag a backer's tweet / Reel; quote-tweet | X + IG |
| Sun | Press hit recap or roadmap teaser | Press logo grid or roadmap visual | LinkedIn + X |

## What to do when nothing is happening (Days 10-14)

The middle of the campaign is the lowest-velocity stretch. Common
symptoms: backer count plateaus, daily pledge drops to $2-3K, replies
slow to a trickle.

This is normal. Do not panic-post.

What works:

1. **Re-share earlier content with a fresh angle**. The launch-day
   posts had 10x the reach they will have in the mid-campaign feed.
   Repost the best-performing launch-day post as a quote-tweet with
   a fresh hook ("11 days ago we launched. Here's where we are now:").
2. **Long-form content**. Mid-campaign is when LinkedIn deep-dives and
   YouTube long-form perform best. The audience that scrolls past in
   week 1 actually reads in week 2.
3. **Press hit translation**. Convert each press hit into 3 social
   posts: the quote pull, the photo, the team reaction.
4. **Outbound DMs**. Reach out to the top 50 most-engaged repliers
   from week 1 with a personal "thanks for being part of this" DM.
   Cost: 30 min. Return: long-tail goodwill + frequent repost
   participation.

What doesn't work:

- Discount tier promotions ("limited 24h discount"). Kickstarter
  policy disallows mid-campaign price drops anyway, but even
  near-misses ("save 10% if you add the keychain") read as desperate.
- Manufactured urgency. The audience can tell.
- Pivoting the story. The Kickstarter page is the contract; don't
  change the value claim mid-campaign.

## Mid-campaign acceptance gate (end of T+21)

- [ ] Funded amount ≥ $750K (typical mid-campaign target if launched at
      $100K week-1 baseline)
- [ ] Backer count ≥ 8,000
- [ ] Stretch crossed: $500K secondary unlock (per
      `../docs/STRETCH_GOALS.md`)
- [ ] At least 2 mid-campaign press hits
- [ ] Reply backlog under 4 hours
- [ ] Community manager has not burned out (check in directly; if
      they're tapped, swap with backup for week 3)

If any gate is red, escalate. Common mid-campaign issues are
recoverable but only if you act before the final 48h.
