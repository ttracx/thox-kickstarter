## Primary personas

### Persona A - The privacy-conscious prosumer

- Demo: 28-45, technical comfort high, lives in a major North American
  or EU metro.
- Pain: refuses to send personal data to OpenAI/Anthropic/Google cloud.
- Wants: a credible local-first LLM-capable device they can carry,
  cluster, or dock.
- Likely entry SKU: ThoxMini ($69) or ThoxAir ($79).
- Channel: organic from Hacker News, Reddit r/LocalLLaMA, HuggingFace.

### Persona B - The educator

- Demo: instructor at K-12, community college, or technical bootcamp.
- Pain: classroom AI use blocked by district / data-handling rules.
- Wants: classroom-safe AI hardware with EDU pricing.
- Likely entry SKU: ThoxMini classroom pack or ThoxAir MagStack cluster
  for cohort use.
- Channel: EDU outreach via EDU_PRICING_LETTER, conference presence.

### Persona C - The maker / hardware tinkerer

- Demo: hobbyist or pro maker active on Tindie, Hackaday, Crowd Supply.
- Pain: existing AI hardware platforms (Jetson, Snapdragon dev kits) are
  expensive and gated by vendor tooling.
- Wants: an open, hackable, Apache-2.0 platform priced for tinkering.
- Likely entry SKU: ThoxClip ($39) or ThoxNova ($499).
- Channel: organic from maker communities, Crowd Supply listing.

### Persona D - The small-business owner

- Demo: 1-10 employees, services or retail.
- Pain: wants AI on the till, on the workshop bench, on the shop floor
  without recurring cloud bills.
- Wants: turnkey on-device AI bundle with clear value-prop per use.
- Likely entry SKU: ThoxNova ($499) plus 1-2 ThoxMinis.
- Channel: small-business skill family integrations, Square / Shopify
  partner mentions.
