# THOX conference decision tree

Operator playbook for attend / exhibit / speak / skip decisions across the 2026 + 2027 conference calendar.

Use this tree BEFORE committing a credit card to any booth, registration, or travel booking. Pair with CONFERENCE_CALENDAR_2026.md for the per-event list.

## First-question funnel

### Q1: Is this a confirmed event with verified 2026 (or 2027) dates and pricing?

- No -> stop. Do not commit. Verify against the official event URL first.
- Yes -> Q2.

### Q2: Is the event audience aligned to a THOX named vertical (developer, EDU, enterprise edge AI, EU regulated industries, retail-hardware)?

- No -> skip unless a strategic reason overrides (e.g., investor density).
- Yes -> Q3.

### Q3: What tier of THOX engagement is justified?

Use the tiered decision rules below.

---

## Tier rules

### Rule 1: Tier-1 tech outlet's home conference

- Examples: Google I/O for Google ecosystem press, Microsoft Build for MS ecosystem press, NVIDIA GTC (competitor) for NVIDIA-orbit press.
- Decision: ATTEND with press in mind. Do NOT exhibit at a competitor's home conference (etiquette violation that reads as desperate or off-brand).
- Lead generation: zero. Press signal: medium. Investor signal: low.

### Rule 2: EDU buyer audience overlaps with THOXKey EDU

- Examples: EDUCAUSE, Texas EdTech Innovation Summit, ISTE.
- Decision: EXHIBIT. EDU is a named THOX vertical and buyers require physical-product trust to convert.
- Lead generation: high. Press signal: medium (trade press). Investor signal: low.

### Rule 3: Conference budget exceeds $10K total commitment

- Decision: DEFER until post-Kickstarter funding visibility (Aug 12 2026 launch + 30-day post-launch readout).
- Recommend exhibit only if Kickstarter raised tier supports it:
  - $250K floor -> skip $10K+ events
  - $500K-$1M base -> approve EDUCAUSE + AI Engineer Summit
  - $1.5M+ stretch -> approve KubeCon NA + Slush + CES 2027
  - $3M ceiling -> approve full slate including TechCrunch Disrupt + VivaTech

### Rule 4: Local Austin event (free or near-free)

- Examples: Capital Factory meetups, Austin Forum, ATX Startup Crawl, EFF Austin happy hours.
- Decision: DEFAULT YES. Marginal cost near-zero. Home-turf compounding value.
- Limit: 8-12 local touchpoints per year to avoid time-dilution.

### Rule 5: Competitor's home conference

- Examples: NVIDIA GTC, Cerebras own events, Groq own events.
- Decision: ATTEND only. Do not exhibit (etiquette + brand-positioning rule).
- Use for: competitive intel, gap analysis, press relationships with reporters covering the space.

### Rule 6: Speaker CFP submission decision

- Default: SUBMIT to every CFP THOX qualifies for. Cost is a slide deck. Reward is multi-week press + free badge + free speaker dinner access.
- Exception: do not submit if the talk would leak unreleased product details or violate compliance posture.

### Rule 7: International travel (non-US events)

- Examples: Slush (Helsinki), Web Summit (Lisbon), VivaTech (Paris), KubeCon EU.
- Decision gate: requires founder-level approval AND demonstrated EU pipeline value AND $1M+ Kickstarter funding.
- Default: attend ONE European event in 2026 (Slush is the highest-leverage pick); reassess for 2027.

### Rule 8: Investor + press density events

- Examples: TechCrunch Disrupt, Slush, Web Summit, Collision.
- Decision: ATTEND if Kickstarter $1M+; EXHIBIT only if Kickstarter $2M+.
- Lead generation: investor-only; not end-user.

### Rule 9: Consumer-electronics retail events

- Examples: CES, NRF Big Show.
- Decision gate: only after Kickstarter delivers and THOX has 1 named retail conversation in motion.
- Default for 2027: CES Eureka Park ONLY if Kickstarter $1.5M+. NRF attend-only.

### Rule 10: Awards + recognition events

- Examples: CES Innovation Awards, Edison Awards, Fast Company Innovation By Design.
- Default: SUBMIT to free + low-cost award programs. Award wins generate disproportionate press for $.

## Per-quarter decision freeze dates

- Q3 2026 commitments: freeze by Jun 30 2026.
- Q4 2026 commitments: freeze by Sep 15 2026 (post-launch visibility).
- Q1 2027 commitments (especially CES): freeze by Oct 15 2026.
- Q2 2027 commitments: freeze by Jan 31 2027.

## Decision-authority matrix

- Local Austin events under $1K: any team member.
- US events $1K-$5K: founder approval.
- US events $5K-$15K: founder + ops approval.
- US events over $15K: founder + ops + advisor review.
- International events: founder + ops + advisor review regardless of cost.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Do NOT commit to events before verifying official 2026/2027 dates.
- Do NOT book non-refundable booth deposits before Kickstarter funding visibility.
- Do NOT split THOX team across too many simultaneous events; quality of presence beats quantity.
- Do NOT exhibit at competitor home conferences.
- Do NOT chase awards for awards' sake; only submit when the award category genuinely fits.
- Do NOT bring child-safety-questionable demos to EDU events (no AI hallucination demos, no off-brand prompts).
