## Tipline patterns

Until the operator pairs this list with their PR tool's reporter database, default to publication-level tiplines. Every modern outlet has a tip channel; pattern varies. Most common:

- `tips@<publication>.com`
- `tip@<publication>.com`
- `news@<publication>.com`
- Web form at `<publication>.com/tips`
- Signal / encrypted tip-channel for investigative outlets (WSJ, Bloomberg, The Information)

**Do not guess.** Visit the publication's "Contact" or "Tips" page and use the published address or form. Tiplines that look right but are not are a fast way to lose access at a publication.

## Reporter-name fill-in

This file deliberately does not list specific reporters. The operator fills in `{{ reporter }}` in the templates by:

1. Reading the byline on the most recent THOX-adjacent story at the target publication (private AI, consumer hardware, Kickstarter campaigns, AI on-device).
2. Cross-checking the reporter's current beat on their masthead bio and on LinkedIn or Bluesky.
3. Confirming the reporter still works at that publication (turnover is high; do not pitch to a reporter who moved 6 months ago).
4. Using the reporter's published contact preference. Many reporters list email and Signal on their bio page.

If no specific reporter can be identified, fall back to the publication tipline.
