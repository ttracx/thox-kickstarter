# tier_3_consumer_tech.md

Mainstream consumer-tech outlets. Pitch with a lifestyle framing on top of the technical product. These outlets reach the broader buyer who has not heard of llama.cpp.

**Lead angle for this tier**: private / on-device AI; "AI that does not leave your desk."
**Recommended template sequence**: initial_outreach -> demo_offer -> follow_up_3_day.

## Embargo policy

Standard 7-day embargo. Tier 3 pitches go at T-14 so the outlet has time to schedule.

## Outlets

---

### Engadget

- **URL**: https://www.engadget.com
- **Beat**: consumer tech reviews, gadgets, smart home, AI hardware. Mainstream voice. Reaches a mass audience.
- **Pitch angle**: family of four devices launching together, starting at $39. Lifestyle framing: "the AI device family you actually own." Lead with the price ladder and the form factor.
- **Contact channel**: `tips@engadget.com`. Reporter-specific: pull byline from recent consumer-AI-hardware coverage.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://www.engadget.com/about/ (contact info; verify)
- **Lead time**: 5-7 days. Engadget runs reviews on a moderate pace.
- **Embargo policy**: accepts 7-day embargoes for hardware launches.
- **Coverage history**: Humane AI Pin, Rabbit R1, Friend pendant, Plaud Note. Engadget has covered the AI-wearable wave; THOX is adjacent.

### Wired

- **URL**: https://www.wired.com
- **Beat**: technology, culture, business. Feature-driven, less news-hit oriented. Long-form lens.
- **Pitch angle**: thesis story. Frame as "the open-source response to closed AI platforms." Founders-from-Texas + Kickstarter + Apache-2.0 as the cultural hook. Wired wants a story, not a spec sheet.
- **Contact channel**: `tips@wired.com`. For feature pitches, identify the staff writer covering AI or consumer hardware via recent bylines.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://www.wired.com/about/ (canonical contact page; verify)
- **Lead time**: 14-21 days for a feature. Wired works on a long calendar.
- **Embargo policy**: yes, for feature pieces. Often interested in a longer pre-brief.
- **Coverage history**: Framework Laptop, System76 Launch keyboard, open-source hardware features.

## Common dos and don'ts for Tier 3

- **Do**: include high-resolution photography in the press kit drop (the render automation pipeline at thox-3dprint-kit/renders/press/ covers this).
- **Do**: lead with a single device that photographs well (ThoxClip is the most camera-friendly).
- **Do**: write the pitch so a non-developer reader gets the value prop in the first paragraph.
- **Don't**: drop into the technical architecture in the first paragraph. Save it for a follow-up.
- **Don't**: bury the price. Tier 3 audiences want to know the price within the first 100 words.
