# tier_6_business_press.md

Business press. Tech beat. Smaller volume of pitches than other tiers but each placement is high signal for the THOX founder narrative.

**Lead angle for this tier**: founder-led from Texas; two-person team; Nevada LLC; Kickstarter funding model; no VC; four devices launching together.
**Recommended template sequence**: initial_outreach -> interview_offer -> follow_up_3_day.

## Embargo policy

Business press accepts embargoes when terms are documented. Pitch goes at T-7.

## Outlets

---

### Wall Street Journal (Technology section)

- **URL**: https://www.wsj.com/news/technology
- **Beat**: technology business reporting. AI industry coverage. Strong on consumer-tech launches and founder profiles when the founder is differentiated.
- **Pitch angle**: founder-led, no-VC, Kickstarter-funded hardware family launching in Austin metro area. WSJ likes a story they can tell to a business audience; the angle is "Texas founders shipping an open-source response to Big AI."
- **Contact channel**: WSJ has a published tips page with secure submission options. Reporter-specific: pull the byline from a recent consumer-tech-launch or AI-startup story. WSJ's technology section publishes contact info per reporter on their masthead bio.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://www.wsj.com/tips (verify current)
- **Lead time**: 7-14 days for a launch news-hit; longer for a feature.
- **Embargo policy**: respects embargoes. Will not pre-print.
- **Coverage history**: Kickstarter consumer-electronics launches with strong founder stories; AI startup launches with funding angles.

---

### Bloomberg (Technology)

- **URL**: https://www.bloomberg.com/technology
- **Beat**: technology and finance. Strong on AI industry shifts, capital flows, founder profiles.
- **Pitch angle**: Kickstarter funding model as an alternative to VC, in a year when VC AI funding is concentrated in a few large rounds. Frame as "the bootstrapped open-source AI hardware story." Mention global shipping availability for the international audience Bloomberg serves.
- **Contact channel**: Bloomberg has a published tips channel including Signal for sensitive tips (not needed here). Reporter-specific: pull byline from recent consumer-AI-hardware coverage.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://www.bloomberg.com/tips (verify)
- **Lead time**: 7-14 days.
- **Embargo policy**: respects embargoes.
- **Coverage history**: AI industry coverage, hardware founder profiles, Kickstarter coverage (less frequent than WSJ).

---

### Forbes (Tech + Under 30 considerations)

- **URL**: https://www.forbes.com
- **Beat**: technology, startups, business, Under 30 lists. Mixed editorial staff + contributor model.
- **Pitch angle**: founder profile angle. If either founder qualifies for an Under 30 list (verify ages internally), the Under 30 editorial path is a separate process from a standard launch pitch. Otherwise, pitch a standard launch story to the tech beat.
- **Contact channel**: Forbes tech beat editors are listed on the masthead. Forbes also has a contributor network; contributor outreach is a separate channel and lower-signal than staff editor outreach.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://www.forbes.com/contact (verify)
- **Lead time**: 7-14 days for staff editor coverage; longer and less certain for contributor coverage.
- **Embargo policy**: respects embargoes for staff editor coverage.
- **Coverage history**: AI startup founder profiles, Under 30 lists, Kickstarter coverage occasionally.
- **Caveat on Forbes contributors**: the contributor network has variable editorial standards. If a Forbes contributor reaches out asking for "pay to play" coverage, decline.

## Common dos and don'ts for Tier 6

- **Do**: lead with the founder story and the funding model. Business press wants the meta-narrative.
- **Do**: have boilerplate company information ready (entity domicile, headcount, funding history, location).
- **Do**: be willing to do a 30-minute phone interview if a reporter responds.
- **Don't**: lead with the product specs. Business press will get to specs in paragraph 4, not paragraph 1.
- **Don't**: claim more than is true about company size or funding. Business reporters verify.
- **Don't**: pay a Forbes contributor for coverage. The practice exists; do not engage with it.
