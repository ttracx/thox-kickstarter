# tier_1_tech_outlets.md

Prosumer + tech press. Highest reach, longest lead times. Pitch first (T-28) so they have room to schedule.

**Lead angle for this tier**: private / on-device AI in a USB-sized device.
**Recommended template sequence**: initial_outreach -> embargo_offer -> demo_offer -> follow_up_3_day.

## Embargo policy

Standard 7-day embargo from receipt of preview. Embargo lifts T-7 (Aug 5, 2026) so coverage publishes the week of launch. State the embargo terms in writing in the initial_outreach template. Confirm acceptance in writing before sending preview material.

### The Verge

- **URL**: https://www.theverge.com
- **Beat**: consumer tech, gadgets, platform politics, "the next computer." Strong on private AI thesis. Has covered Kickstarter hardware launches before (Playdate, Framework, etc.).
- **Pitch angle**: private / on-device AI in a USB-sized device. Frame as "AI that does not leave your desk." Lead with ThoxClip + THOXKey form factor.
- **Contact channel**: `tips@theverge.com` is the standard tipline. For reporter-specific outreach, pull the byline from a recent consumer-AI-hardware story and use the contact email on that reporter's masthead bio. Many Verge reporters list Signal numbers for sensitive tips; the launch is not sensitive so email is fine.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://www.theverge.com/contact (canonical contact page; check for current link before send).
- **Lead time**: 7-10 days for a considered review; 3-5 days for a news-hit at launch. Tier-1 pitch goes at T-28 so the reporter has time to negotiate the embargo internally.
- **Embargo policy**: typically accepts embargoes if terms are in writing. Will publish at embargo lift to the minute. Will not honor an embargo offered after they have already started reporting.
- **Coverage history with analogous products**: Framework Laptop launch (laptop modularity), Playdate (handheld console Kickstarter), Rabbit R1 (AI hardware skepticism). The Verge has been openly skeptical of "AI in a box" devices that promise more than they deliver; address that head-on by emphasizing what THOX runs locally today, not what it aspires to.

## Common dos and don'ts for Tier 1

- **Do**: include the Kickstarter pre-launch URL in every pitch so the reporter can land directly on the pre-launch page.
- **Do**: name both founders (Tommy Xaypanya + Craig Ross) in the first paragraph so the reporter knows who they can interview.
- **Do**: offer the demo unit. Tier 1 reporters expect hands-on access for a hardware review.
- **Don't**: send to two reporters at the same publication in the same week. It looks desperate and the internal triage will route both copies to the same desk anyway.
- **Don't**: claim coverage from a competing publication. Reporters do not care; they may care negatively.
- **Don't**: lead with the funding goal in the subject line. Lead with the product or the angle.
