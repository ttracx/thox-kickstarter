## Tier 1: Edge-AI / privacy-tech specialists

### Profile

- Fund size: $50M to $500M AUM, often Fund I or Fund II in current vintage
- Sector thesis: edge AI, on-device inference, privacy-by-design, open
  source AI infrastructure
- Stage match: pre-seed to Series A
- Geography: US (SF Bay, NYC, Boston, Austin, Seattle), EU (London, Berlin,
  Paris), select Toronto/Montreal funds
- Check size for THOX round: $250K to $2M
- Typical lead check: $750K to $1.5M
- Pro-rata expectation: light (less than 25 percent reserve)

### Why THOX fits

- Privacy-by-design is THOX's first-principles thesis, not a marketing line.
  An edge-AI specialist fund will recognize the difference.
- Hardware-plus-software combination is a category many of these funds have
  thesis-mapped but rarely seen executed (most edge-AI plays are
  software-only).
- Apache-2.0 software stack on a public repo graph (github.com/ttracx) is
  due-diligence-friendly for funds with technical partners.

### Sample firms (thesis verification recommended before any outreach)

Identify funds whose published thesis explicitly mentions one or more of:

- Local AI compute
- On-device inference
- Edge AI hardware
- Privacy-preserving ML infrastructure
- Open-source AI tooling
- Consumer hardware that runs ML workloads

Verify the thesis match by reading:

- The fund's About page or Thesis page
- The general-partner blog posts in the last 12 months
- The fund's recent portfolio additions (last 6 months)
- The fund's announced funds (Fund I, II, III, etc.) to confirm AUM and
  vintage

Do NOT fabricate fund names. Do NOT pitch a fund whose published thesis
does not match THOX's category. Pitching off-thesis wastes both sides'
time and burns the warm-intro channel.

### Recommended ask (Tier 1)

- $500K to $1M lead check
- 1 to 2 co-investors per round of $250K to $500K each
- Round size at lead-plus-co totals $1M to $2M

### Intro hop count

- 1 to 2 warm hops typical
- A Tier-1 fund will almost always require a warm intro for a hardware
  pre-seed; cold outreach has near-zero conversion at this tier

## Tier 2: Hardware + community-funded crossover

### Profile

- Fund size: $25M to $200M AUM
- Sector thesis: hardware companies that grew from Kickstarter or
  Indiegogo into scaled businesses (Pebble, Oculus pre-Facebook, Anker
  early stages, Glowforge, Prusa-class makers, sub-$500 consumer
  electronics with passionate communities)
- Stage match: post-campaign Series A bridge
- Geography: US (Boulder, SF Bay, NYC, Pittsburgh, Austin), select
  Shenzhen-adjacent funds
- Check size: $500K to $3M
- Typical lead check: $1M to $2M

### Partner profile

- Often former operators or founders themselves (Kickstarter alumni,
  Y Combinator alumni who built hardware, ex-Pebble, ex-Glowforge, ex-
  Formlabs, ex-Anker, ex-Bose, ex-iRobot, ex-Roku)
- Hardware-aware due diligence: they ask about BOM, contract manufacturer,
  certification, fulfillment ops, returns rate, channel mix
- Patient on hardware timelines: a typical hardware product has 6 to 12
  months between funded and shipping; software-only funds find this
  uncomfortable

### Why THOX fits

- THOX is post-campaign by the time this tier engages, so the unit-economics
  story is data-backed
- The 30+ public repos give the technical partners a complete due-diligence
  package they cannot get from most hardware companies
- The four-SKU launch with a common runtime is a category-creation pitch
  this tier appreciates more than a single-device pitch

### Recommended ask (Tier 2)

- $1M to $2M lead check
- $500K to $1M of follow-on reserve
- Round size at lead-plus-follow: $2M to $4M
- Post-money valuation negotiable based on Kickstarter result (single
  largest input)

### Intro hop count

- 1 to 2 warm hops typical
- Successful Kickstarter alumni in this fund's portfolio are the highest-
  conversion warm-intro source

## Tier 3: AI + open-source / community-aligned

### Profile

- Fund size: $50M to $300M AUM
- Sector thesis: open-source AI infrastructure, community-friendly licenses
  (Apache-2.0, MIT, BSD-3), developer-tool plays, infrastructure-as-product
- Stage match: pre-seed to Series A
- Geography: US (SF Bay, NYC), EU (Berlin, London, Stockholm), select
  Tel Aviv funds
- Check size: $250K to $2M
- Typical lead check: $500K to $1.5M

### Sample thesis match

- "We invest in open-source AI infrastructure" (explicit thesis-page text)
- "We back companies that build with the community, not against it"
- "Apache-2.0 / MIT-licensed projects with commercial runway"
- "Edge inference, privacy by architecture, on-device ML"
- "Hardware companies that ship open software"

### Why THOX fits

- Apache-2.0 across the THOX repo graph is a hard differentiator versus
  proprietary edge-AI competitors
- THOX has actual community surface (30+ public repos, public CI, public
  release notes, public migration docs) rather than a single open-source
  badge
- The MagStack Cluster Dock as an open-print kit (ttracx/thox-3dprint-kit)
  is a tangible community-builder this tier understands

### Recommended ask (Tier 3)

- $500K to $1M follow check (Tier 3 funds rarely lead hardware rounds)
- Tier 3 is the highest-volume co-investor tier (3 to 5 funds at
  $250K to $500K each is common)

### Intro hop count

- 1 to 2 warm hops typical
- Open-source contributor channels (project maintainers, conference
  speakers, podcast hosts) are the highest-conversion warm-intro source

## Tier 4: Strategic + corporate VC

### Profile

- Fund size: typically off-balance-sheet (corporate venture arm)
- Sector thesis: aligned with the parent corporation's product roadmap
  (cloud AI providers, chip manufacturers, hardware OEMs, consumer
  electronics retailers, telco arms with edge-compute strategies)
- Stage match: Series A and later (rarely pre-seed)
- Geography: global (corporate parents are global)
- Check size: $1M to $10M
- Typical lead check: $2M to $5M

### Sample thesis match (by parent category, not specific arms)

- Semiconductor chip company VCs: edge compute, on-device inference,
  silicon-friendly AI workloads
- Cloud AI provider VCs: hybrid cloud-plus-edge plays, on-device privacy
  tier complementing cloud premium tier
- Consumer electronics OEM VCs: hardware design, fulfillment scale,
  retail channel
- Telco VCs: edge compute at the carrier edge, low-latency on-device AI

### Why THOX fits

- THOX is a strategic accelerant for a chip VC: their silicon runs our
  inference; their roadmap benefits from a high-volume independent OEM
- THOX is a complementary strategic for a cloud AI VC: on-device privacy
  tier extends the cloud customer's portfolio without cannibalizing
  premium cloud workloads

### Pros of Tier 4

- Largest check sizes available
- Strategic resources (supply chain introductions, certification
  acceleration, retail channel access)
- Validation effect: a corporate-VC name on the cap table is a hiring and
  partnership signal

### Cons of Tier 4

- Long decision cycles (3 to 9 months typical, versus 2 to 8 weeks for
  Tier 1)
- Competitive sensitivity: a corporate VC's parent may launch a competing
  product after seeing the data room
- Strategic-VC term sheets often carry ROFR, MFN, and information-rights
  clauses that complicate Series B and beyond
- LP base may include strategic-rationale-only investors who are not
  pure-financial; this can create misaligned exit pressure later

### Recommended ask (Tier 4)

- Strategic follow check ($1M to $3M) AFTER a Tier-1 or Tier-2 lead is
  committed
- Do NOT lead with a Tier-4 fund: the timeline mismatch will stall the
  round
- Negotiate ROFR / MFN / info-rights down to standard pro-rata if at all
  possible; attorney must review before signature

### Intro hop count

- 1 to 3 warm hops typical
- Best warm-intro source: a Tier-1 or Tier-2 fund's portfolio company that
  already took a strategic check from the same arm

## Out-of-scope categories

These categories are NOT on the target list for this round. Reasons listed
below each.

### Generalist consumer VCs

- Reason: THOX is a deep-tech hardware play with an open-source software
  thesis. Generalist consumer VCs typically lack the technical due-
  diligence depth and price the round wrong (either over-anchored on
  hardware-as-CPG margins or under-priced on the software optionality).

### Crypto / Web3 funds

- Reason: THOX has no token, no on-chain primitive, and no decentralized
  network thesis. Pitching a crypto fund on THOX wastes their time and
  ours.

### Late-stage growth funds

- Reason: stage mismatch. Revisit at Series B or later.

### Family offices without verified tech-investment history

- Reason: pre-seed / seed family offices often look attractive on the
  surface but bring inconsistent decision processes and information
  expectations. Family offices with verified tech-investment history are
  fine; unverified family offices are not.

### Funds in jurisdictions THOX cannot legally accept money from

- Reason: defer to attorney + CPA for the specific list. As of this
  draft, OFAC-sanctioned jurisdictions and any fund with significant LP
  exposure to a sanctioned jurisdiction are out of scope.

## Operator workflow

For each Tier-1 candidate you want to engage:

1. Run the candidate through `INVESTOR_SCREENING_FRAMEWORK.md`.
2. If combined score is above the threshold, run the candidate through
   `WARM_INTRO_BUILDER.md` to identify a warm-intro path.
3. If a warm-intro path exists, send a first-touch via the path (NOT a
   cold DM to the partner).
4. If a meeting is scheduled, prepare with `PITCH_MEETING_PLAYBOOK.md`.
5. If the meeting closes positively, follow up within 24 hours.
6. If the candidate is a soft pass, log the reason in your private CRM-lite
   and move on. Do NOT re-engage the same fund within 90 days unless a
   material change to THOX's story justifies it.

For each Tier-4 candidate:

1. Defer engagement until a Tier-1 or Tier-2 lead is committed.
2. Use the Tier-1/Tier-2 lead as a warm-intro source to the Tier-4 arm.
3. Same screening + meeting workflow as above; longer timeline (90+ days
   expected).
