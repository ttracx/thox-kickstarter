# TechCrunch Disrupt

## Overview

- Typical dates: late spring or fall 2027 (Disrupt schedule has shifted; verify against techcrunch.com)
- Location: typically San Francisco (Moscone West)
- URL: https://techcrunch.com/events/tc-disrupt
- Audience: startup founders, VCs, growth-stage operators, TechCrunch press, corporate-venture
- Size: ~10K attendees; high investor density per square foot

## Why THOX

TechCrunch Disrupt is the canonical US startup investor + press event. THOX's Kickstarter delivery story (assuming on-time fulfillment) is exactly the narrative TechCrunch loves to write about. Disrupt is where THOX should make the transition from "Kickstarter darling" to "Series A candidate" in the press narrative.

Disrupt's Startup Battlefield is high-leverage if THOX qualifies; even reaching the application round generates press.

## ROI angle

- Primary: investor introductions. Target 15+ qualified Series A / late-seed VC conversations.
- Secondary: 5-10 TechCrunch + tier-1 tech press conversations.
- Tertiary: corp-dev conversations for acquisition-optionality awareness.
- Startup Battlefield: if accepted, the application + finals generate multi-week press regardless of outcome.

## Decision criteria

- Default decision: exhibit at Startup Alley if Kickstarter $1M+.
- Hard skip if Kickstarter under $750K (Disrupt without funding traction reads as desperate).
- Startup Battlefield application: always submit; the cost is time + a slide deck.
- Decision gate for upgrade to premium booth: $2M+ funded.

## Budget breakdown

- Conference registration: ~$2K-$4K founder pass per attendee
- Startup Alley booth: $5K-$15K
- Booth furniture + AV: $1-3K
- Travel: $1K Austin-to-SF + lodging $300-$500/night
- Per-diem: $150/day SF conference week
- Collateral: $1K printed (investor 1-pager critical), $2-4K THOXKey giveaway (50-100 units)
- Total envelope: $10K (attend + 1 staff) to $25K (Startup Alley + 2 staff)

## Booth / demo configuration

- SKUs to demo: full SKU lineup with hero-product front and center (depends on Kickstarter sales leader)
- Live demo: 30-second "what does it do" loop tuned for VC + press attention spans
- Signage: investor-tuned. Lead with traction numbers (Kickstarter backers + dollars raised + units shipped) not technology.
- Handout: 1-page investor brief + 1-page traction-metrics sheet
- Lead capture: VC opt-in flow separate from THOXKey-end-user opt-in flow

## Startup Battlefield

If THOX qualifies (typical criteria: <$5M funding raised, public launch within the year):
- Application deadline typically 2-3 months before event; verify
- Strong THOX angles: "we hit $X on Kickstarter without a Series A" + "we shipped on time" + "we built private AI hardware"
- Pitch deck: 6 slides max; lead with one big surprising number

## Speaker / panel submission

- Disrupt has both editorial-curated stages and open CFP stages
- THOX talk title placeholders:
  - "How we turned a Kickstarter campaign into a hardware company"
  - "The case for shipping AI in hardware, not in the cloud"
  - "Lessons from delivering 10,000 USB AI accelerators"
- Theme fit: Disrupt favors data-rich + opinionated founder narratives. Lead with numbers.

## Networking strategy

- Disrupt parties are dense; pick 2-3 high-signal vendor + VC parties max
- Target meet list: 15 named VCs (mix of seed + Series A leads), 5 named corp-dev leads, 5 named tech press
- Pre-Disrupt 1:1 booking via warm intros is higher leverage than walk-up booth conversations
- Use Brex Founders dinner or similar pre-event dinners if invitable

## Lead capture

Two-tier opt-in. THOXKey end-user opt-in (standard flow) + separate VC / press opt-in (LinkedIn-pull + manual qualification).

## Post-event nurture

- T+24h: VC + press thank-you with traction-metrics one-pager
- T+7d: investor deck send to any active VC conversation
- T+14d: schedule 1:1 calls for any active VC conversation
- T+30d: Series A conversation kickoff for any qualified VC
- T+60d: press follow-up cycle for any active press lead
