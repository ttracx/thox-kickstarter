## Escalation criteria

A partnership conversation escalates from "we are talking" to "we are signing" when ALL of the following are true:

1. The counterpart has named a single point of contact responsible for the THOX relationship
2. The counterpart has provided written acknowledgment of the asks in the brief (even if the answer is "no" on some asks)
3. THOX engineering has validated the technical surface area required for the partnership (e.g. adapter shape, model-card behavior, runtime fingerprint)
4. Tommy + Craig have both reviewed the latest brief and agreed to advance

Until ALL four are true, the conversation stays at the discovery-call tier.

## Volume math

Across all 8 briefs, expect:

- 2-3 productive Tier 1 conversations (Anthropic, OpenAI, Cohere are all credible; one or two will return real engagement; OpenAI is the most likely to ghost)
- 1 productive Tier 2 conversation (Gemma DevRel is responsive)
- 1-2 productive Tier 3 conversations (Hugging Face is the most likely to engage; NVIDIA Jetson is post-launch only)
- 0-1 productive Tier 4 conversations (Apple is post-launch only; Qualcomm is post-launch only)

Plan for 4-7 real partnership conversations to surface from the 8-brief slate over the first 90 days post-launch. Not all of those will become formal partnerships. Some will become "we acknowledge each other on a docs page" relationships, which is fine.

## Hard rules

1. NEVER fabricate executive names in any outreach. Use role-level addressing ("partnership lead", "developer-relations lead") if the actual name is not known.
2. NEVER cold-email a press desk for a partnership ask. Wrong inbox.
3. NEVER offer THOX hardware or services in exchange for press coverage. That is pay-to-play and torches credibility.
4. NEVER send the same brief to multiple companies. Each brief is per-company customized.
5. NEVER commit THOX to anything beyond a discovery call without Tommy + Craig sign-off.
6. NEVER agree to NDAs that block THOX from talking about the partnership publicly or that block THOX from continuing to support other vendors.
7. NEVER spend money on paid introductions, paid placement, or paid partnerships pre-launch.
8. NEVER let a partnership conversation slow down the Aug 12 launch. Launch is the priority. Partnerships are second-tier.
