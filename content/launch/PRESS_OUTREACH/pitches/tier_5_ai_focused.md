# tier_5_ai_focused.md

AI industry publications, newsletters, and podcasts. Smaller per-outlet audience than Tier 1, but the audience is the right audience: people who shape the AI conversation.

**Lead angle for this tier**: private / on-device AI as the thesis; THOX as evidence the form factor for owned-AI is forming.
**Recommended template sequence**: initial_outreach -> interview_offer (for podcasts) -> embargo_offer.

## Embargo policy

Variable. Substack-based newsletters (Import AI, AI Snake Oil) often publish on a fixed weekly cadence; embargo timing must align with their publish day. Podcasts (Latent Space, Last Week in AI) record on a calendar; pitch as an interview offer.

### Last Week in AI

- **URL**: https://lastweekin.ai
- **Format**: weekly podcast + newsletter. Hosts cover top AI news items each week.
- **Pitch angle**: launch is news; pitch as a news item with one-paragraph summary and a link. Optional: founder availability for a 5-10 minute interview segment.
- **Contact channel**: contact form on the site; check the about page for current email. Substack-based newsletter side accepts press emails.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://lastweekin.ai/about (verify current contact)
- **Lead time**: 5-7 days; the show records weekly, so embargo timing must align with their cadence.
- **Embargo policy**: respects embargoes when terms match their publish schedule.
- **Coverage history**: every notable AI launch; on-device AI threads frequently.

### Latent Space

- **URL**: https://www.latent.space
- **Format**: podcast + newsletter. Hosts swyx and Alessio interview AI builders. High-quality long-form conversation.
- **Pitch angle**: founder interview. Tommy + Craig on the show talking about the on-device AI thesis, the 7-backend router architecture, and why open-source plus owned hardware is the right pair.
- **Contact channel**: contact via the site's about / pitch page (verify current channel). Hosts are active on X / Bluesky / LinkedIn; direct outreach there is acceptable for a well-targeted pitch.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://www.latent.space (verify pitch form or contact link)
- **Lead time**: 21-30 days. Podcast bookings have a long calendar.
- **Embargo policy**: less relevant for podcast format; episode publishes on the show's calendar.
- **Coverage history**: AI infrastructure, model serving, open-source models, AI hardware founders.

### Stratechery

- **URL**: https://stratechery.com
- **Format**: subscription analysis newsletter by Ben Thompson. Long-form strategic analysis of tech industry shifts.
- **Pitch angle**: thesis story. Frame THOX as evidence in a larger argument about the platform shift from cloud AI to on-device AI. Ben writes about the shape of the industry, not individual product launches; pitch the trend, not the product.
- **Contact channel**: contact form on the site. Stratechery does not solicit product pitches; the play is to make the operator's pitch one data point in a thesis Ben is already developing.
- **Submission guidelines**: https://stratechery.com/about/ (verify)
- **Lead time**: 30+ days, and coverage is not guaranteed. Treat as opportunistic.
- **Embargo policy**: not the right framing for this outlet.
- **Coverage history**: Apple Silicon, Anthropic / OpenAI strategy, the post-cloud platform thesis.

### AI Snake Oil (Princeton; Arvind Narayanan + Sayash Kapoor)

- **URL**: https://www.aisnakeoil.com
- **Format**: Substack newsletter focused on separating AI hype from substance. Academic-leaning.
- **Pitch angle**: only pitch if there is a credible substance-over-hype story. THOX claims on-device inference; provide reproducible benchmarks (the thoxllm-factory eval suite is available). If the pitch is honest about limitations, this audience may engage; if the pitch is marketing-puffed, it will be ignored or worse.
- **Contact channel**: Substack reply-to email. Authors are public on X / Bluesky.
- **Submission guidelines**: no formal process.
- **Lead time**: variable. The newsletter publishes on its own schedule.
- **Embargo policy**: not typically relevant.
- **Coverage history**: critical analyses of AI product claims; occasionally positive coverage of substance-grounded launches.

## Common dos and don'ts for Tier 5

- **Do**: lead with the thesis (private AI, on-device, owned hardware). Tier 5 audiences buy theses; they tolerate products.
- **Do**: provide a reproducible technical artifact (the GitHub repo, the eval suite, the benchmark dataset).
- **Do**: offer the founder for a podcast interview where the format fits.
- **Don't**: send hype-y copy to AI Snake Oil. It will backfire.
- **Don't**: pitch Stratechery a product. Pitch a trend.
- **Don't**: pitch Latent Space without confirming the host's current scheduling cadence.
