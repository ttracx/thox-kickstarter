# 00_master_voice_guide.md

Master voice and pacing guide for every unboxing script in this directory.
Read this before you read any per-device script. Every per-device script
assumes the rules below.

## Pacing

- Target speech rate: about 150 words per minute. That is the natural
  founder cadence on the existing 90-second hero. Faster than 170 wpm
  reads as anxious. Slower than 130 wpm reads as scripted.
- Pause at natural breath beats, not at punctuation. A comma in writing
  is not a pause in voiceover unless the breath needs it.
- For a 30-second cut, plan about 75 spoken words plus 5 seconds of
  silence and ambient sound design.
- For a 45-second cut, plan about 110 spoken words plus 6 to 8 seconds
  of silence.
- For a 60-second cut, plan about 150 spoken words plus 8 to 10 seconds
  of silence.

## Tone

- Technical, honest, no marketing fluff.
- Lead with what the device does, not what it makes the user feel.
- Quote specific numbers and specific component names. "Eight seconds to
  boot" beats "boots fast." "Luckfox Pico Mini B" beats "powerful SoC."
- Acknowledge limits when they exist. "Two nodes today. Four if you go
  higher" is better than "scales infinitely."
- No superlatives. No "amazing", "revolutionary", "game-changing",
  "world's first", "best-in-class". The hardware speaks for itself.

## Founder split

- Craig Ross leads hardware narratives. Industrial design, assembly,
  enclosure, port layout, magnets, LED behavior, physical scale, the
  feel of the device in hand.
- Tommy Xaypanya leads software and AI narratives. Boot behavior,
  THOXCore routing, on-device inference, MagStack clustering, skills,
  privacy posture, the Kickstarter price and date.
- Both founders close the hero video. For the per-device unboxings, one
  founder leads the narrative and the other delivers the closing CTA.
  Default: Craig opens, Tommy closes. Exception: ThoxNova and MagStack
  Cluster Dock close on Craig because the assembly story is the hero.

## Dos

- Use precise numbers and real component names.
- State real wall-clock claims you can demonstrate on camera (boot
  time, snap time, LED color).
- Show the device doing the thing the script claims. If the script
  says the LED bar lights cyan on first boot, the cut must include
  the LED bar lighting cyan on first boot.
- Use "we" when referring to THOX. Use "you" when referring to the
  backer.
- Use the THOX brand cyan `#27E5FF` as the only saturated rim light
  in frame unless the script calls for magenta `#FF3DA8`.

## Don'ts

- No superlatives (see Tone above).
- No vague benefits ("changes everything", "unlocks possibilities").
- No em-dashes in subtitles or captions. Use a period or a comma.
- No emojis in subtitles, captions, or social copy.
- No exclamation marks anywhere.
- No competitor names, visible or spoken.
- No medical, financial, or safety claims.
- No promises beyond what shipped. If a feature is planned but not in
  the production firmware, do not show it on camera and do not name it
  in the voiceover.
- No "support us." The outro is always "Back us" or "back us on
  Kickstarter."

## Outro convention

Every unboxing cut closes on the same structural beat:

1. The THOX logo overlay fades in on a clean cut to the device.
2. The Kickstarter link or campaign URL appears under the logo in
   IBM Plex Sans Medium.
3. Voiceover delivers the closing CTA. The script for each SKU
   specifies who delivers it.
4. The closing CTA always uses one of these two phrasings:
   - "Back us on Kickstarter. August twelve."
   - "Back us. August twelve. Kickstarter."
5. The final frame holds for 1.5 seconds in silence.

The price callout in the CTA varies by SKU and is specified in each
per-device script. The price callout always uses the spoken form
("seventy-nine dollars") in voiceover and the symbol form ("$79") in
the overlay graphic.

## Asset hygiene

- Audio: mic close, dry, no room reverb. Apply a single subtle pad in
  the music bed for cohesion across the campaign.
- Visual: dark walnut desk surface, plain background, brand-cyan rim
  light. Founders in plain dark tops. No patterns, no logos other than
  the THOX wordmark on the device itself.
- Cuts: prefer hard cuts on the beat. No cross-dissolves except into
  the final logo card.

## Subtitle rules

- Word-for-word match to the voiceover. No paraphrasing.
- Plain language. No em-dashes (use a period or comma). No emojis. No
  exclamation marks.
- Two lines maximum per caption. Break at natural phrase boundaries.
- Use the THOX brand foreground color `#F2F4F8` on a 60% black
  background bar pinned to the lower third.

## Compliance gate

Every cut goes through founder review before posting. See `README.md`
"Quality gate" for the review path.
