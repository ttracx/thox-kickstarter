# DevReady Austin (local meetups + small conferences)

## Overview

- Typical cadence: quarterly meetups + occasional 1-day mini-conferences throughout 2026 + 2027
- Location: Austin, Texas (Capital Factory, Galvanize, various coworking + meetup venues)
- URL: varies; coordinate via Austin tech meetup networks + Capital Factory calendar
- Audience: Austin tech community, indie devs, local founders, Capital Factory residents
- Size: 50-300 attendees per event; very high local-density value

## Why THOX

DevReady (and the broader Austin local-meetup scene) is the free + high-leverage home-turf play. THOX is Austin-based; every conversation costs zero travel + zero per-diem + builds local-press + local-VC + local-talent relationships that compound over multiple years.

Treat the local Austin tech-meetup scene as a recurring touchpoint, not a single event. Aim for THOX presence at 8-12 local touchpoints across 2026.

## ROI angle

- Primary: local hiring funnel. Austin tech talent attends these events; THOX can recruit without a recruiter spend.
- Secondary: local press warmth (Austin Inno, Austin Business Journal, Austonia, Austin Monitor).
- Tertiary: Capital Factory network depth (introductions to TX VCs, regional corp-dev).
- Quaternary: rehearsal venue for new talks before bigger conferences.

## Decision criteria

- Default decision: yes to almost every Austin meetup with credible audience.
- Marginal cost is near-zero; marginal value compounds.
- Hard skip only if the meetup audience is mismatched (e.g., crypto-only, MLM, anti-aligned content).

## Budget breakdown

- Conference registration: $0-$50 per event (meetup pricing)
- Booth: typically free or low-cost ($0-$500 for a table at a 1-day mini-conf)
- Travel: $0 (local)
- Per-diem: $0 (drive home)
- Collateral: $200 per event for printed cards + $500-$1K per event for THOXKey giveaway (10-25 units per event)
- Annual envelope: $5K-$10K total across 8-12 Austin local touchpoints

## Booth / demo configuration

- Bring 1-2 demo SKUs per event (no full SKU lineup; meetups are too small)
- Live demo: 5-minute self-running loop on laptop showing THOXKey + MagStack
- Signage: small tabletop sign + 1-page handout
- Lead capture: simple email signup + THOXKey serial-number giveaway

## Speaker submission

- Most Austin meetups are first-come-first-served for speaker slots
- THOX talk title placeholders:
  - "How we built a hardware startup in Austin without leaving Austin"
  - "Shipping local-first AI: an Austin case study"
  - "Open source supply chains: lessons from a Kickstarter campaign"
- Theme fit: Austin meetup audiences reward war-story authenticity over polished pitches. Be honest about what is broken.

## Networking strategy

- Capital Factory member events (if member; if not, prioritize membership for $X/month)
- Austin Forum talks (broader audience including non-tech)
- EFF Austin happy hours (privacy-aligned audience matches THOX brand)
- Austin Tech Council events
- Austin Latino Tech Society, Austin Black Tech Society events for community depth

## Lead capture

THOXKey serialized opt-in but with a lower bar; local-community opt-ins are more relaxed than enterprise events.

## Post-event nurture

- T+24h: thank-you only to event organizer (not full attendee list; respect meetup norms)
- T+7d: invite to private THOX Austin Slack channel if relevant
- T+30d: include in monthly Austin newsletter recap
- T+90d: re-engagement at the next Austin event
