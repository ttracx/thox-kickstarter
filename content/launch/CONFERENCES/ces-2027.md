# CES 2027 (Las Vegas)

## Overview

- Typical dates: early-to-mid Jan 2027 (verify against ces.tech)
- Location: Las Vegas Convention Center + Venetian Expo
- URL: https://www.ces.tech
- Audience: consumer-electronics buyers, retailers (Best Buy, Target, Costco buyers attend), global press, brand partnership leads
- Size: ~115K+ attendees; the single largest consumer-electronics event globally

## Why THOX

CES is the hardware-showcase event. If THOX intends to be perceived as a consumer hardware brand and not only a developer / B2B brand, CES presence is the line.

CES is also where retailers do their next-year buying conversations. Best Buy, Target, Costco buyers attend. A booth here gates many big-box retail conversations for 2027.

CES is the single highest-cost event on the THOX calendar and the decision to commit must be funding-gated.

## ROI angle

- Primary: retail buyer conversations. Target 5-10 named big-box + specialty retail buyer conversations.
- Secondary: 20+ press conversations (CES is press-saturated; THOX needs a sharp angle to cut through).
- Tertiary: 5+ international distributor conversations (CES is the global expo; Asian + EU distributors attend).
- CES innovation award submission: high-leverage; award winners get disproportionate press for $.

## Decision criteria

- Hard decision gate: Kickstarter raised $1.5M+ to justify exhibit budget
- Eureka Park (startup zone) is the right tier for THOX year-one; do not chase main-hall booths
- Hard skip if Kickstarter under $1.5M; attend without booth instead
- Eureka Park booth decision must be locked by Oct 2026 (booths fill early)

## Budget breakdown

- Conference registration: ~$300-$1K exhibitor passes + ~$300 attendee passes
- Eureka Park booth (startup zone): $5K-$8K small footprint
- Main hall small booth: $20K-$40K
- Booth furniture + AV + carpet + electrical + drayage: $5K-$10K (CES nickel-and-dimes mercilessly)
- Travel: $800-$1.2K per person + lodging $400-$800/night Vegas conference week
- Per-diem: $175/day Vegas conference week
- Collateral: $2K printed, $5-10K THOXKey giveaway inventory (200+ units serialized)
- Shipping booth materials: $2-5K
- CES Innovation Award entry fee: ~$500-$1K per category
- Total envelope (Eureka Park, 3 staff, 4 nights): $25K-$50K

## Booth / demo configuration

- SKUs to demo: full THOX SKU lineup (ThoxClip, ThoxMini, ThoxAir, ThoxNova, MagStack)
- Live demo: MagStack physical-clustering reveal moment (snap 4 nodes together, watch capacity scale) is the hero demo
- Secondary demo: THOXKey USB privacy story with WireShark visualization
- Tertiary demo: child-safe THOXKey EDU station with parents-and-kids interaction
- Signage: brand-system-compliant (#0B1220 + #27E5FF), bold typography readable at 50 ft
- Handout: 4-page printed product catalog
- Lead capture: serialized THOXKey opt-in (CES leads are noisy; opt-in is the only signal worth nurturing)

## Press strategy

- CES is press-saturated; THOX needs a sharp angle
- Pitch: "the Kickstarter darling that delivered on time"
- Target embargo: 1 Tier-1 tech outlet (Verge, Wired, or Engadget) gets first look 48h before show floor opens
- CES press conference: only worth holding if THOX has 1 newsworthy announcement (e.g., a named retail partnership signing at CES)

## Speaker submission

- CES typically has very few open CFP slots; most main-stage speakers are paid sponsorships
- Skip CES speaking; focus on showroom + retail buyer meetings

## Networking strategy

- Eureka Park startup events (free + valuable for peer founders)
- C Space (digital + marketing track) parties for press exposure
- ShowStoppers and Pepcom pre-show events (these are press-only and require RSVP)
- Target meet list: 5 named retail-channel buyers + 5 named distributor leads

## Lead capture

CES leads are noisy. Filter brutally. Only nurture opt-ins tagged with named retailer / distributor / press affiliation. Discard general curious-attendee leads.

## Post-event nurture

- T+24h: thank-you only to qualified press + retail leads
- T+7d: retail-buyer proposal package for any active conversation
- T+14d: 1:1 retail-buyer calls
- T+30d: ship sample SKUs to qualified retail buyers
- T+90d: retail-purchase-order conversation for any active relationship
